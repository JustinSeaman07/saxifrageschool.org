Woodshop Primer and Box-Building

About the Class

Learn safe and expert use of our woodshop's tools and practice your skills building the most useful of items: a Box! Special emphasis in this course will be placed on teaching students to utilize tools and simple/pratical methods with basic tools they can cheaply acquire and use after the course is complete. Students will be expected to obtain a small set of inexpensive hand tools prior to class attendance.

Primary tools included:

  • chopsaw and circular saw
  • router
  • bandsaw
  • drill press and drilling jigs
  • chisels
  • Japanese hand saws, etc.

Primary course outcomes:

  • Joinery
  • Chiselwork and sharpening
  • Squaring and prepping lumber
  • Gluing up

Box-Project Options (any box you'd like!)

  • Beehive
  • Amplifer Case
  • Toolbox
  • Planter Box
  • Crate
  • Any average-sized box

About the Instructor

Wade Caruso has worked as a designer and carpenter for over 10 years. After graduating with a degree in Landscape Architecture, he worked for a number of years for a green building company learning sustainable construction techniques, timber framing, and solar panel installation. More recently, he has worked as a independent contractor doing custom renovation projects and has a small business designing and fabricating custom furniture pieces. Wade specializes in fabrication projects using salvaged materials.
