Maps and GIS (Hybrid)

About the Class

(Above: The Saxifrage School Campus Map)

In this experimental class, you will engage in a hybrid learning experience that brings together the best of online learning and the best of real world practice. Students will register and fully participate in the upcoming Coursera© class "Maps and the Geospatial Revolution" while meeting in-person with our expert instructor and fellow students to contextualize and implement their mapping skills through project-based learning. The online course is 5 weeks, followed by 5 weeks of project-based learning, and a feast in week 11.

What you get:

1. Coursera Support

Our expert instructor will be there to guide, encourage, and challenge you as you go through Anthony C. Robinson's 5-week Coursera© class. The group will meet on a weekly basis during the Coursera© class to work collaboratively and receive supplementary instruction. Runs July 23rd to August 13th

Class will meet on Tuesday evenings from 7:00 - 9:30, location is tba.

2. Five weeks of Project-Based Learning

Work with our instructor to take what you have learned from the Coursera class and refine your skills by working on some real mapping and GIS projects here in Pittsburgh. Your project will benefit the work of a local community organization. Runs August 20th to September 17th

Class will meet on Tuesday evenings from 7:00 - 9:30, location is tba.

3. Map Geek Feast

To conclude our class, we'll geek out about all the map skills we've learned and share a fancy meal with some of the region's best map experts. It's a chance to eat and celebrate the completion of the course and gain some valuable advice from mapping mentors. It will be part casual conversation, part dinner guest lecture.

The feast will be held on Saturday, September 14th. Location is tba.

Details: $249 Tuition (plus $50 admin fee).

If you have already registered, go directly to the payment page.

About the Instructor(s)

Evaine Sing is a registered Landscape Architect with a BLA from Virginia Tech and a Masters in Public Policy & Management at the Heinz College of Carnegie Mellon University. She works at gtech, focusing on Community and Economic Development as it relates to the issue of vacant land management within the urban context, primarily looking at ways to engage individuals to take ownership of their surrounding environment and creative, sustainable solutions that serve as a catalyst to larger transitional changes. In addition to using mapping tools for many years professionally, Evaine has helped instruct multiple GIS and mapping courses at CMU and CCAC.
